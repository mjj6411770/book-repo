About the Work

The Green Substrate is a philosophical inquiry into the living ground that underlies consciousness, ecology, and the structures of meaning we build upon them. It asks what remains when we strip away assumption — and what grows from what remains.

Part meditation, part argument, the manuscript moves between the rigor of analytic philosophy and the openness of phenomenological reflection, seeking a language adequate to the entanglement of mind and world.

Excerpt

"Beneath every system of thought there is a substrate — not inert, but alive, green, growing. We do not think upon solid ground. We think upon something that thinks back."

— from The Green Substrate

About the Author

A thinker drawn to the edges where philosophy meets the living world. The Green Substrate is the product of years of reflection on what it means to build meaning on ground that is itself meaningful.
